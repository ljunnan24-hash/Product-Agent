SignalShelf interview notes

Interview sample: 12 early-stage builders, mostly technical founders or maintainers.

Repeated pains:
- 7 people said they collect feedback but do not trust their own prioritization.
- 6 people said they only review signals right before a launch or investor update.
- 5 people said GitHub issues and Discord messages are hard to compare with analytics.
- 4 people said they wanted a "weekly digest" but not another dashboard.
- 3 people said they would rather see uncomfortable counter-evidence than motivational summaries.

Positive reactions:
- "If this tells me what not to build this week, that is useful."
- "I do not want a CRM. I want a product judgment log."
- "A local-only version is easier to try with messy notes."

Objections:
- "This sounds like something I can do with a spreadsheet."
- "I do not want to paste private customer data into a random cloud app."
- "Weekly summaries are nice, but I need the source quotes nearby."
- "I would not pay until it connects to GitHub or Discord."

Pricing signals:
- 3 people said they might pay 10-20 USD/month for a solo product.
- 2 agency operators said client reporting would be worth more if evidence export looked professional.
- 5 people were unsure because the workflow is not yet a habit.

Suggested experiments:
- Run a concierge version for 5 builders for 3 weeks.
- Compare memo quality against a manually written founder update.
- Test a GitHub-only workflow for open-source maintainers.
- Ask users to mark which memo claims they trusted and which they ignored.
